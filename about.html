About BLEprint

A more intelligent way to revisit what a wall used to look like.

BLEprint is being built to help construction teams transform build-time progress photos into a field-ready visual reference for hidden infrastructure.

Core idea

Use the documentation teams already capture to make later work safer, faster, and less uncertain.

The problem

Crews often make high-risk decisions without visibility into what is hidden.

Utility strikes and accidental damage happen when finished walls conceal pipes, wiring, and other systems. The cost shows up in rework, delays, and avoidable safety risk.

What we learned

Construction teams already capture useful information, but it is rarely organized for later use.

Through conversations with workers, managers, and site leaders, we found that progress photos are common, yet the information usually stays buried in folders instead of becoming a reliable decision tool.

The direction

BLEprint focuses on software-first visibility instead of adding more hardware to the jobsite.

The goal is a mobile experience that helps people pull the right historical view at the right moment, then align that record to the wall before the work begins.

How it works

A simple flow, built around the construction timeline
