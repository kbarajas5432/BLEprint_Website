About BLEprint

A software-first way to help construction teams visualize hidden infrastructure and reduce costly mistakes on site

The Problem

Construction accidents and utility strikes happen when teams do not know what is behind walls. These mistakes can damage pipes and cables, delay projects, and create major direct costs.

Our Solution

BLEprint is a phone app that lets users upload progress photos during build time and later create an AR overlay on the wall, helping users understand what is hidden behind the surface before starting work.

What We Learned

Through interviews with construction workers, our team learned that utility strikes are extremely costly, current options are often unreliable or require hardware, and people already take progress photos but do not use them in a consistent or real-time way.

We also learned that managers and site overseers valued this idea even more than direct workers. That pushed BLEprint toward a software-first direction focused on helping site leaders make safer and more informed decisions.

BLEprint is being built to turn existing construction documentation into a practical tool that helps reduce accidents, prevent delays, and make job sites safer.

How It Works

Capture during construction. Use later during installation, maintenance, and renovation.

BLEprint starts with progress photos collected throughout the construction process. Those photos are tagged and organized so that when a user returns to a wall later, the app can pull the relevant images and overlay them in context. The goal is to help teams reference hidden infrastructure before drilling, cutting, or opening a surface.
